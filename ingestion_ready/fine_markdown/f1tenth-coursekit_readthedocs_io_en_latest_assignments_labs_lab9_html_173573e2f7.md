# Lab 9 - Robot Ethics

**Note:** This is an **INDIVIDUAL** assignment.

**Tip:** Be prepared for the discussion in [Lecture 22](../../lectures/ModuleF/lecture22.html#doc-lecture22).

**Goals:**

As engineers, we design systems for specific functions, such as moving parts in a warehouse, interpreting a surgeon's hand movements, or driving passengers. Beyond functionality, we also incorporate safety, security, and reliability. With autonomous systems, we must also design _ethics_ into them. These systems will coexist with us and make autonomous decisions, some of which will inevitably involve ethical questions. How can we design and program an ethical autonomous system? What does it mean for an autonomous system to be ethical or responsible for its actions? Are there autonomous systems that cannot be ethical? When designing ethical systems, _whose ethics are we considering_?

**Learning Outcomes:**

This assignment prompts a discussion on the ethical considerations in designing autonomous systems, not limited to self-driving cars. It is divided into three parts:

1.  Who or what is morally responsible for the decisions made by an autonomous system?
2.  How can responsibility be programmed?
3.  How do context and function affect the design of ethical behavior?
