## 18TH RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition

The RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition is a semi-regular event organized by an international community of researchers, engineers, and autonomous systems enthusiasts. Teams participating in the 18th RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition at IV 2024 will build a 1:10 scale autonomous race car according to a given specification and develop software to achieve the competition objectives: Don’t crash and minimize lap time.

IV 2024 will host an in-person competition. The main focus is on physical cars, with each team bringing their own RoboRacer vehicle and writing its software. Teams have access to a detailed build manual and can utilize open-source software to get started. The organizers provide the race setup (rules, submissions, guidelines), the track, and related infrastructure, and manage the race itself.

For the competition, teams first participate in a qualification session to determine seeding. This session involves a time trial where teams must demonstrate they can complete laps without colliding with the track boundaries. Following qualifications, teams compete in a knockout phase, racing head-to-head with their autonomous driving algorithms. As vehicles and hardware are standardized, teams must develop robust perception, planning, and control algorithms capable of handling the uncertainties of a new track and new competitors. The RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition phase pits competitors against each other on the same track.
