## RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition Resources

### Build

The RoboRacer Autonomous Vehicle System is an open-source platform for autonomous systems research and education on a 1:10 scale. It serves as the baseline for the in-person competition, providing sensors and computation power for autonomous driving algorithms. Participants in the in-person competition must bring their own RoboRacer vehicle. Detailed build instructions, including videos and step-by-step guidance, are available at: [Roboracer Build Instructions](https://f1tenth.org/build.html).

### Simulation

Extensive simulation is crucial for developing and evaluating autonomous driving algorithms before deploying them on a physical vehicle. RoboRacer offers various simulation environments:

*   **Roboracer Gym:** Used for the virtual competition, this asynchronous, 2D simulator is built in Python. It runs faster than real-time (30x), offers realistic vehicle simulation and collision detection, supports multiple vehicle instances, and publishes laser scan and odometry data. Available at: [Roboracer Gym](https://github.com/f1tenth/f1tenth_gym).
*   **Roboracer ROS Simulator:** Provides a simulation environment that mirrors the ROS messages from the RoboRacer Vehicle, suitable for more in-depth vehicle development. Available at: [Roboracer ROS Simulator](https://github.com/f1tenth/f1tenth_simulator).

### Autonomous Racing

For those new to autonomous racing, the following learning resources are available:

*   **Roboracer Learn:** Complete course material from Prof. Rahul's lectures can be found online. This course covers autonomous driving foundations, includes tutorials on the RoboRacer Vehicle, and explores autonomous racing techniques like raceline finding. Access it at: [Roboracer Learn](https://f1tenth.org/learn.html).
*   **RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Course (YouTube):** All lectures from the course have been recorded and are available on YouTube: [RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Course](https://youtu.be/zENhppcxwzY).
