# RoboRacer Sim Racing League @ ICRA 2026

## About

**RoboRacer Autonomous Racing** is a competition organized by researchers, engineers, and autonomous systems enthusiasts. The teams participating in the **27th RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition** at [ICRA 2026](https://2026.ieee-icra.org) will write software for a 1:10 scaled autonomous racecar. The objective is: **_drive fast but don’t crash!_**

This is the fifth **RoboRacer Sim Racing League**, which uses [AutoDRIVE Ecosystem](https://autodrive-ecosystem.github.io) to simulate a RoboRacer racecar on a virtual racetrack.

The Sim Racing League is a virtual competition accessible globally. For the [ICRA 2026](https://2026.ieee-icra.org) competition, each team will receive a standardized simulation setup: a digital twin of the RoboRacer vehicle and the Porto racetrack within the [AutoDRIVE Simulator](https://github.com/Tinker-Twins/AutoDRIVE/tree/AutoDRIVE-Simulator). Teams will also be provided with a working [AutoDRIVE Devkit](https://github.com/Tinker-Twins/AutoDRIVE/tree/AutoDRIVE-Devkit). Teams must develop perception, planning, and control algorithms to process real-time sensor data from the simulator and generate control commands for the simulated vehicle.

The competition has 2 stages:

*   **Qualification Race:** Teams must complete multiple laps around a practice track without colliding with track bounds.
*   **Time-Attack Race:** Teams compete against the clock on a new racetrack to achieve a top leaderboard position.

Since the vehicle, sensors, simulator, and devkit are standardized, teams must develop robust racing algorithms capable of handling uncertainties on unseen racetracks.

If you are interested in autonomously racing physical RoboRacer vehicles, see the [27th RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition](https://icra2026-race.roboracer.ai) website, held in person at [ICRA 2026](https://2026.ieee-icra.org). You can register for both physical and virtual competitions.

Summary of events:

*   **Registration:** Teams register for the Sim Racing League.
*   **Qualification Round:** Teams demonstrate successful completion of 10 laps around a provided practice track.
*   **Qualification Results:** Standings of qualified teams are released.
*   **Competition Track:** Organizers release the "competition track" for the final race.
*   **Final Race:** Organizers collect containerized algorithms and run them in the simulator. Performance metrics are recorded.
*   **Competition Results:** Standings for the final race are released.

The RoboRacer Sim Racing League will be held approximately one week before [ICRA 2026](https://2026.ieee-icra.org). Discussions are ongoing with the ICRA organizing team to allow teams to present their approaches/results in a short session at [ICRA 2025](https://2026.ieee-icra.org).

## Resources

[AutoDRIVE](https://autodrive-ecosystem.github.io/) is an open ecosystem for autonomous driving research and education. It connects software simulation with hardware deployment through the [AutoDRIVE Simulator](https://github.com/Tinker-Twins/AutoDRIVE/tree/AutoDRIVE-Simulator) and [AutoDRIVE Testbed](https://github.com/Tinker-Twins/AutoDRIVE/tree/AutoDRIVE-Testbed). It also provides the [AutoDRIVE Devkit](https://github.com/Tinker-Twins/AutoDRIVE/tree/AutoDRIVE-Devkit) for developing autonomy algorithms. For the Sim Racing League, teams will use the AutoDRIVE Devkit to interface with the AutoDRIVE Simulator.

[RoboRacer](https://roboracer.ai) is an international community focused on converting 1:10 scale RC cars into autonomous vehicles for research and education. Refer to the [documentation](https://roboracer.ai/build) to build your own RoboRacer. The open-sourced [course material](https://roboracer.ai/learn) is available for those new to autonomous racing. You can also explore [research](https://roboracer.ai/research) enabled by RoboRacer and check out physical [RoboRacer races](https://roboracer.ai/race). While a physical RoboRacer vehicle is not required for the Sim Racing League, the learning resources are valuable for understanding autonomous racing fundamentals.

## Submission

Each team must submit a containerized version of their autonomous racing software stack. Submissions for each phase are handled separately.

Upon running your submitted container, all necessary nodes should start automatically. When the "Connection Button" on the AutoDRIVE Simulator's "Menu Panel" is pressed, the simulated vehicle should begin running. Ensure all necessary commands (sourcing workspaces, setting environment variables, launching nodes) are included within the `entrypoint` script (`autodrive_devkit.sh`) provided in the `autodrive_roboracer_api` container. Do **NOT** use `~/.bashrc` or similar methods for automation. Organizers must be able to start additional bash sessions within your container without your codebase executing automatically. Include detailed instructions on your [Docker Hub repository overview](https://docs.docker.com/docker-hub/repos/manage/information#repository-overview) for downloading and running your container image.

Use the secure form below for Phase 1 (Qualification Round) submissions. Provide your team's name and the link to your Docker Hub repository. If using a private repository, add [autodriveecosystem](https://hub.docker.com/u/autodriveecosystem) as a [collaborator](https://docs.docker.com/docker-hub/repos/manage/access/#collaborators).
