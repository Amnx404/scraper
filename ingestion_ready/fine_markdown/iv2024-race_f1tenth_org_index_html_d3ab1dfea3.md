## 18TH RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition

The RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition is a semi-regular event organized by an international community of researchers, engineers, and autonomous systems enthusiasts. Teams participating in the 18th RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition at IV 2024 will build a 1:10 scale autonomous race car according to a given specification and develop software to achieve the competition's objectives: Don't crash and minimize lap time.

IV 2024 will host an in-person competition. The primary focus is on physical cars, with each team bringing their own RoboRacer vehicle and writing its software. Teams will have access to a detailed build manual and can utilize open-source software to facilitate their start. The organizers will provide the race setup (rules, submissions, guidelines), the track, and related infrastructure, and will manage the race itself.

For the competition, teams will first participate in a qualification session to determine seeding. This session involves a time trial where teams must demonstrate their ability to complete laps without colliding with the track boundaries at runtime. Following qualifications, teams will compete in a knockout phase, racing head-to-head with their autonomous driving algorithms. As vehicles and hardware are standardized, teams must develop robust perception, planning, and control algorithms capable of handling the uncertainties of a new track and new competitors. The RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition phase pits competitors against each other on the same track.
