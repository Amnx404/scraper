# Call for Contribution:

This workshop investigates learning and decision-making in complex high-dimensional cooperative-competitive multi-agent systems. The focus is on settings where games may not be zero-sum, environments may be dynamic and only partially known, and objective functions may be non-stationary and non-convex. The workshop aims to identify meaningful solution approaches for such settings and the conditions under which solutions exist. Additionally, it seeks to develop approaches for designing tractable algorithms with provable convergence to near-optimal solutions and guarantees.

We invite papers related to the following topics. Position papers, work in progress, and novel but not necessarily thoroughly worked-out ideas are encouraged. Submissions will be reviewed by the workshop's program committee and will undergo a thorough review process, receiving 2-3 high-quality reviews.

### Topics of Interest

*   Game-theoretic approaches
*   Multi-agent reinforcement learning
*   Multi-agent coordination
*   Multi-agent simulation and modeling
*   Scaling multi-agent systems
*   Decentralized learning and control
*   Sim-to-real transfer in multi-agent systems
*   Multi-agent systems that adapt to safety constraints
*   High-performance, agile robots in cooperative and competitive settings
*   Tools for addressing multi-agent challenges on real-world robots
