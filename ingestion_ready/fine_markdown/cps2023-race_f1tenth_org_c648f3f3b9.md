## 12th F1TENTH Autonomous Grand Prix

F1TENTH Autonomous Racing is a competition organized by researchers, engineers, and autonomous systems enthusiasts. Teams participating in the 12th F1TENTH Grand Prix at CPS-IoT 2023 will build a 1:10 scaled autonomous race car and write software for it to "Don’t crash and minimize laptime."

At CPS-IoT 2023, an in-person competition with physical cars will be hosted. Each team will bring their own F1TENTH car and develop its software. Teams will have access to a detailed build manual and can utilize open-source software to aid their development. The organizers will provide the race setup (rules, submissions, guidelines), the track, and related infrastructure, and will manage the race itself.

A new feature for the CPS-IoT 2023 competition is a track with branching paths offering different traction properties. Teams must equip their autonomous cars with the ability to choose between high-traction, low-risk, longer paths and low-traction, high-risk, shorter paths.

For the competition, teams will first participate in a qualification round to determine seeding. Qualifying evaluation includes two tasks: a time trial on a track without obstacles, and finishing laps on the same track with unknown and potentially moving obstacles without collision. This is followed by a multi-car tournament seeded by the time trial leaderboard from qualifying. The final tournament will consist of a sequence of races with multiple cars, where the best-placing cars advance to subsequent rounds. The ordering of cars to races will be determined by qualifying lap times.
