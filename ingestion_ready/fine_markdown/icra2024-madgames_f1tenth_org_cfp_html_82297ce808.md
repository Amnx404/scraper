# Call for Contribution:

This workshop aims to investigate learning and decision-making in complex, high-dimensional cooperative-competitive multi-agent systems. This includes scenarios where games may not be zero-sum, environments might be dynamic and only partially known, and objective functions could be non-stationary and non-convex. The workshop seeks to identify meaningful solution approaches for such settings and the conditions under which solutions exist. Additionally, it encourages the development of approaches for designing tractable algorithms with provable convergence to near-optimal solutions and guarantees.

We invite papers related to the following topics. Position papers, works in progress, and novel but not necessarily thoroughly worked-out ideas are encouraged. Submissions will be reviewed by the workshop's program committee and will undergo a thorough review process, receiving 2-3 high-quality reviews.

### Topics of Interest

*   Game-theoretic approaches
*   Multi-agent reinforcement learning
*   Multi-agent coordination
*   Multi-agent simulation and modeling
*   Scaling multi-agent systems
*   Decentralized learning and control
*   Sim-to-real transfer in multi-agent systems
*   Multi-agent systems that adapt to safety constraints
*   High-performance, agile robots in cooperative and competitive settings
*   Tools for addressing multi-agent challenges on real-world robots

### Submission Format

Papers should be 4-6 pages, including references, in PDF format, using the standard [IEEE ICRA Conference template](http://ras.papercept.net/conferences/support/support.php). Accepted papers will be made available on the website. Authors are invited to give an oral (live) presentation or a video highlight.
