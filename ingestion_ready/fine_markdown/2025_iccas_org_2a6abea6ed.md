# General Information

## Climate & Weather

Incheon has a continental climate with oceanic influences due to its proximity to the sea, resulting in a relatively stable annual temperature range. The annual average temperature is around 12.1°C. The all-time high was 38.9°C (August 16, 1949), and the all-time low was -21.0°C (January 11, 1931).

For weather and forecasts, visit: <http://english.visitkorea.or.kr/enu/AK/AK_EN_1_1_2.jsp>

## Korean Food

Korean cuisine is known for its nutritious, well-balanced, and low-calorie dishes, featuring a wide variety of vegetables and seasonings like garlic, red pepper, scallion, soy sauce, fermented bean paste, ginger, and sesame oil. Visitors can also find American, Continental, Chinese, and Japanese restaurants.

Specific culinary experiences include black pork barbecue, horse meat, and seafood, particularly on Jeju Island. [View Details](http://www.visitjeju.net/en/index.jto?menuCd=DOM_000001818001000000)

## Currency

The currency in Korea is the won.
*   **Notes:** 1,000 won, 5,000 won, 10,000 won, 50,000 won
*   **Coins:** 10 won, 50 won, 100 won, 500 won

### Cash & Check

Foreign banknotes and traveler's checks can be exchanged for Korean won at foreign exchange banks and authorized money exchange outlets. Exchange rates are subject to market fluctuations.

### Credit Card

Major credit cards such as VISA, American Express, Diners Club, Master Card, and JCB are accepted at hotels, department stores, restaurants, and retail outlets.

## Business Hours

*   **Government Offices:** Weekdays, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m.
*   **Banks:** Weekdays, 9:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. (Closed Saturdays and Sundays)
*   **Major Department Stores:** Daily, 10:30 a.m. to 7:30 p.m.

## Tip

Tipping is not a standard practice in Korea. Service charges are typically included in bills at hotels and upscale restaurants. Tipping may occur for exceptional service.

## Insurance

Participants are advised to arrange their own travel and health insurance. The Organizing Committee does not provide insurance coverage for participants and is not liable for any accidents.

## Electricity

Korea uses both 110 and 220-volt electrical systems, with 220 volts being more common, especially in newer hotels and residences. It is recommended to verify the voltage beforehand.

## Time Difference

Korea is 9 hours ahead of Greenwich Mean Time (GMT+9).
