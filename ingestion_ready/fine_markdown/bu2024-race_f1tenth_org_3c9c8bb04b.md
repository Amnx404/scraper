## BU F1TENTH AUTONOMOUS GRAND Prix

F1TENTH Autonomous Racing is a competition organized by an international community of researchers, engineers, and autonomous systems enthusiasts. The BU F1TENTH Grand Prix is organized by faculties and students at Boston University and is part of the [Learning to Trust Autonomy Workshop](https://sites.bu.edu/learningautonomy/).

Participating teams build a 1:10 scaled autonomous race car according to a given specification and write software for it with the objectives: Don’t crash and minimize laptime.

Boston University (BU) will host an **in-person** competition with physical cars. Each team brings their own F1TENTH car and writes the software. Teams have access to a detailed build manual and can use open-source software. The organizers provide the race setup (rules, submissions, guidelines), the track, and related infrastructure.

The competition includes a qualification session for seeding, where teams compete in a time trial to demonstrate they can finish laps without colliding with track bounds. This is followed by a knockout phase of head-to-head racing. Since vehicles and hardware are standardized, teams must develop robust perception, planning, and control algorithms to handle uncertainties of a new track and competitors.
