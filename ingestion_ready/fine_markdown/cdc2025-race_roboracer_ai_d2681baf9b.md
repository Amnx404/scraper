# 25TH RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition

**2025 IEEE Conference on Decision and Control (CDC 2025)**
December 10th - 12th, 2025
Windsor Convention Center, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil

## 25TH RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition

RoboRacer Autonomous Racing is a competition organized by an international community of researchers, engineers, and autonomous systems enthusiasts. Teams participating in the 25th RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition at CDC 2025 will build a 1:10 scaled autonomous race car according to a given specification and write software for it to fulfill the competition objectives: Don’t crash and minimize laptime.

CDC 2025 will host an in-person competition featuring the 25th RoboRacer Autonomous Racing Competition. The main focus is on physical cars. Each team will bring their own RoboRacer car and develop its software. Teams have access to a detailed build manual and can utilize open-source software to get started. The organizers provide the race setup (rules, submissions, guidelines), the track, and related infrastructure, and they organize the race itself.

For the competition, teams first participate in a qualification session to determine seeding. In this time trial, teams must demonstrate their ability to finish laps without colliding with the track bounds at runtime. Afterwards, teams compete in a knockout phase, racing head-to-head with their autonomous driving algorithms. Since vehicles and hardware are standardized, teams must develop robust perception, planning, and control algorithms capable of handling the uncertainties of a new track and new competitors. The Racing Competition phase pits competitors against each other on the same track.
