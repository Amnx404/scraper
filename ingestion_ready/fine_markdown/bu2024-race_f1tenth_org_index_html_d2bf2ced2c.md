## BU F1TENTH AUTONOMOUS GRAND PRIX

F1TENTH Autonomous Racing is a competition organized by an international community of researchers, engineers, and autonomous systems enthusiasts. The BU F1TENTH Grand Prix is organized by faculty and students at Boston University and is part of the [**Learning to Trust Autonomy Workshop**](https://sites.bu.edu/learningautonomy/).

Participating teams build a 1:10 scaled autonomous race car according to a given specification and write software for it to fulfill the competition objectives: Don’t crash and minimize laptime.

Boston University (BU) will host an **in-person** competition with physical cars. Each team brings their own F1TENTH car and software. Teams have access to a detailed build manual and can use open-source software. The organizers provide the race setup (rules, submissions, guidelines), the track, and related infrastructure.

The competition includes:
*   **Qualification Session:** A time trial to determine seeding, where teams must demonstrate they can finish laps without colliding with track bounds.
*   **Knockout Phase:** Head-to-head racing where autonomous driving algorithms compete.

Since vehicles and hardware are standardized, teams must develop robust perception, planning, and control algorithms to handle uncertainties of new tracks and competitors. The Grand Prix phase pits competitors against each other on the same track.
